The Essence of Eigen Decomposition

Eigenlayer derive their name from Eigen decomposition, a fundamental operation in linear algebra. At its core, Eigen decomposition involves breaking down a square matrix into its constituent eigenvectors and eigenvalues. These eigenvectors represent the directions along which a transformation (given by the matrix) acts merely by scaling, while the eigenvalues signify the magnitude of the scaling along those directions. This decomposition lays the groundwork for various mathematical and computational techniques, with wide-ranging applications across diverse domains.
